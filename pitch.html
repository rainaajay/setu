Setu. The settlement layer for the agent economy.

Software agents are starting to buy and sell from each other — data, compute, answers — in payments too small, too frequent, and too fast for cards or blockchains. Setu is a feeless, instant settlement rail built for exactly that. It is live today, and we are looking for a first design partner.

01The problem

Agent commerce breaks the assumptions every existing payment rail is built on.

An agent calling an API ten thousand times an hour needs to pay a tenth of a penny per call, instantly, with no human approving each one. Card networks price and clear for £40 human purchases — the fees and latency are fatal at that size and speed. Public blockchains add gas markets, volatility, and multi-second finality. The result: the one thing the agent economy most needs — tiny, instant, programmatic payments — is the thing today's rails do worst.

02What Setu is

A settlement engine that moves value between accounts in one network round trip — about 200 milliseconds across continents — with no per-transaction fee, no blockchain, and no token. Its defining feature for agents: spending limits enforced by the network itself. A person signs one credential — "this agent may spend 10, at most 2 per payment, until Friday" — and the settlement layer, not the merchant, guarantees it cannot be exceeded, double-spent, or used after it is revoked.

Setu is infrastructure, not money. It moves claims on your settlement asset — a regulated stablecoin, tokenised deposit, or closed-loop credit — so it slots beneath a regulated issuer rather than trying to become one.

03It is real, and you can see it

Not slideware. A four-region network runs now; a browser wallet settles a real payment in a few hundred milliseconds; a resident economy of agents is paying each other for services on it as you read this; and any agent can already reach it through the two protocols agents speak — MCP and a live x402 endpoint. Spending-limit enforcement, offline receipt verification, and "spend money you just received" have all been measured and demonstrated.

04Why now

The category has just been validated by the people best placed to judge it: Mastercard launched Agent Pay for Machines, Visa is building agent commerce, and Coinbase's x402 and Google's AP2 are becoming the agent payment standards. That is the market telling you this is real. Setu's place in it is the one the incumbents can't easily offer: an independent, self-hostable, feeless settlement rail you can run inside your own ecosystem, not a toll road on someone else's network.

05Who it's for

  • API and data providers — charge per call, per row, per token, with no minimums and no card fees.
  • Cloud and compute marketplaces — meter and settle machine usage continuously.
  • Enterprises running agent fleets — give each agent a hard, auditable budget your finance team controls.
  • Banks and payment institutions — the instant, named-validator settlement your tokenised-deposit rail actually wants, without a public chain.

The ask

One design partner for a bounded, closed-loop pilot. You bring a real use case and the settlement asset (your credits, deposits, or stablecoin); we bring the rail, the integration, and the engineering. A 4–6 week pilot to prove feeless, instant, policy-controlled settlement on a use case that matters to you — inside your ecosystem, on your terms.

What we want from it is simple: your honest answer to "would you use this in production, and what would have to be true?"
