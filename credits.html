Issuance & redemption terms

Setu Credits.

What a unit on the Setu network is — the concrete answer to the white paper's central question (§6–§7): what does the number represent?

A Setu Credit is not money. Not a bank deposit, stablecoin, e-money, or security; not a claim on any currency; not redeemable for cash. On the current network it is a test credit with no monetary value.

What it is

A Setu Credit is a closed-loop credit issued by the operator of the Setu network. It is redeemable for one thing: services offered by participants within the Setu economy — like a platform credit or an arcade token, useful inside the ecosystem and nowhere else.

Issuer

Every Credit is issued by a single named account, the Treasury, controlled by the operator. Every Credit in circulation traces back to a Treasury issuance — no anonymous or hidden minting. The Treasury address and total supply are published and auditable on the network.

Supply

The supply is fixed at genesis. The Treasury is created with a defined number of Credits and issues from that pool; it does not mint new Credits afterwards. "Circulating" Credits are those the Treasury has issued and not yet had returned. (Demonstrated in src/demo-issuer.ts: the sum of all holdings always equals the fixed supply.)

Issuance

On the testbed: the Treasury grants Credits on request (the faucet); they are free test credits, worthless outside the demo. In a real closed-loop deployment: Credits would be issued against defined consideration — granted for services rendered, or purchased within a specific commercial ecosystem whose operator is the acknowledged issuer. In a regulated-money variant (future): Credits would be a beneficial claim against money held by a licensed issuer, per white paper §7 — out of scope here and not claimed.

Redemption

A Credit is used by spending it for a service inside the economy, and may be returned to the Treasury. There is no promise to redeem a Credit for cash or any external asset. Holding a Credit is holding the ability to pay for in-network services, nothing more.

Wind-down

If the testbed is retired, Credits cease to have any function. Because they were never money and carried no external claim, there is nothing to redeem and no one is owed anything.
